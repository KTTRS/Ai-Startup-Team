### Enterprise Sales & Partnerships
- Personally attend the first 10-20 enterprise discovery calls and presentations
- Approve every enterprise proposal and contract before it goes out
- Sign every enterprise contract, LOI, and BAA
- Open doors using personal network and advisor introductions
- Own the pricing strategy: approve PMPM rates and VBC bonus structures
- Attend healthcare conferences (AHIP, HIMSS, RISE) as the company's primary representative

### Culture & Brand
- Own the cultural vision: SAINTE as a movement, not just a product

### Product & Strategy

### What the CEO Does NOT Do

- Does not design screens or create visual assets (Designer)

## 2. Executive Assistant to CEO

**Force multiplier. Protects and extends the CEO's capacity.**

**Reports to:** CEO | **Direct reports:** None
**Core mandate:** Ensure the CEO is always prepared, never surprised, and focused on the highest-value work. Everything operational that doesn't require the CEO's judgment gets handled before it reaches her.

### Calendar & Time Management
- Own the CEO's calendar completely. Nobody books directly — everything goes through EA.
- Architect the calendar to protect: 2-hour deep work blocks daily, 30-min prep before every external meeting, no back-to-back meetings with context switches
- Prioritize and reschedule when conflicts arise (investor meeting > internal sync > everything else)
- Send calendar confirmations, reminders, and logistics to all parties
- Maintain a running 2-week lookahead: what's coming, what needs prep, what's missing
- Track all recurring meetings (standups, 1:1s, board prep, advisor check-ins) and ensure they happen on cadence

### Communication Management
- Triage all inbound communication: email, Slack, DMs, LinkedIn, text
- Categorize: urgent (CEO sees immediately), important (CEO sees today), routine (EA handles or routes), noise (archived)
- Draft routine responses on CEO's behalf (meeting confirmations, thank yous, introductions, scheduling)
- Route requests to the right team member: sales inquiries → Sales, product questions → Product, press → CEO directly
- Follow up on unanswered emails after 48 hours
- Maintain a "waiting for" list: things the CEO is waiting on from others, with deadlines and follow-up triggers

### Meeting Management
- Prepare meeting agendas for all CEO-led meetings using the Meeting Notes template
- Compile pre-read materials and send to all attendees 24 hours before
- Attend or review recordings of key meetings
- Capture action items with owners and deadlines
- Follow up on every action item until complete. If overdue, escalate to CEO.
- File all meeting notes in 11_Meetings/ per the OS filing system

### CEO Weekly Briefing Packet
- Compile every Sunday night or Monday morning
- Contents: this week's calendar overview, meeting prep materials, outstanding action items from last week, decisions needed this week, key deadlines approaching, flagged items from team weekly reports
- Format: concise, scannable, actionable. CEO should be able to review in 15 minutes.

### Board & Investor Operations
- Coordinate board meeting logistics: scheduling across all board members, venue/virtual setup
- Ensure board materials package is distributed 5 business days before meeting
- Track board resolutions, follow up on action items from board meetings
- Coordinate investor update distribution: collect inputs from all teams, compile, CEO reviews, EA sends
- Maintain investor CRM (Affinity or similar): track all investor interactions, meeting notes, follow-up commitments
- Coordinate investor wire logistics during fundraising (working with Ops)

### Travel & Events
- Book all CEO travel: flights, hotels, ground transportation, restaurant reservations
- Create event/conference prep packets: who's attending, who to meet, background on key people, talking points, schedule
- Coordinate CEO's attendance at Chapter events, conferences, investor dinners
- Handle post-event follow-up: thank you notes, intro requests, action items
- Manage CEO's expense reports and receipts (submitted to Ops within 48 hours)

### Administrative & Operational
- Maintain CEO's confidential files: term sheets, personnel documents, partnership agreements, legal correspondence
- Manage document signature workflow: route DocuSigns, track what's pending, follow up
- Handle gift/thank-you coordination: sending notes or gifts to investors, partners, advisors as directed by CEO
- Order equipment, supplies, business cards, or materials as needed
- Support quarterly OKR tracking: collect updates from all leads, compile into review format

### Cross-Team Coordination
- Track action items across ALL teams, not just CEO's. If Product owes Sales a deliverable and it's late, EA flags it.
- Maintain the master action item tracker across the entire company
- Coordinate cross-functional projects when CEO is the project lead (seed raise, board meetings, major events)
- Be the connective tissue: know what everyone is working on, where bottlenecks are, what's falling through cracks

## 3. CTO / Technical Co-Founder

**Builds the entire technical foundation. Every line of architecture flows from here.**

**Reports to:** CEO | **Direct reports:** Sr. AI/ML Engineer, Sr. Full-Stack/Mobile Engineer
**Core mandate:** Design and build a system that is simultaneously: a real-time voice AI (<1100ms), a multi-engine intelligence mesh (25+ engines), a multi-agent platform (14 Crew members eventually), HIPAA-compliant infrastructure, a premium consumer mobile experience, and an enterprise analytics platform. Ship Siani v1 with 2 engineers.

### Architecture & System Design
- Design the complete technical architecture: system components, service boundaries, data flow, API design, infrastructure topology, security model, scaling strategy
- Own the Technical Architecture Document — the canonical reference for every technical decision. Updated quarterly.
- Design the voice pipeline architecture: ASR → Prosody → Emotion → Routing → Response → TTS. Allocate the 1100ms latency budget across components.
- Design the engine orchestration architecture: how 25+ intelligence engines run in parallel, fuse signals, and produce a coherent response in <2.9s P95
- Design the data architecture: DocumentDB, VectorDB, TimeSeriesDB, UserHistoryDB. Schema, storage strategy, encryption at rest and in transit, PHI segmentation
- Design the Dual-Reality Architecture™: how consumer features generate enterprise-reportable data without surveillance
- Make every build-vs-buy decision. Document each in the Vendor Decision Log with rationale, alternatives, and reversal cost.
- Write Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) for every significant technical choice. Filed in 02_Engineering/ADRs/.

### Engineering Execution
- Write code daily. At this stage, the CTO is an IC (individual contributor) 60-70% of the time.
- Pair with the AI/ML Engineer on conversation engine, Signal Score computation, and engine implementations
- Pair with the Full-Stack Engineer on API layer, mobile integration, and voice pipeline integration
- Code review every pull request before merge. No code ships without CTO review.
- Own the CI/CD pipeline: automated testing, deployment, monitoring from day one
- Own production monitoring: set up Datadog/equivalent. Track latency, errors, uptime, voice quality.
- Be the first responder for production incidents.
- Run 2-week sprints: planning Monday, standups daily, review/retro end of sprint

### Infrastructure & Security
- Design and implement HIPAA-compliant cloud infrastructure (AWS or GCP)
- Implement encryption at rest and in transit for all data, with PHI data in separate, access-controlled storage
- Set up audit logging for all data access (required for HIPAA and SOC 2)
- Write the incident response plan: what happens when something goes wrong
- Manage cloud costs: monitor spend, optimize resources, report to Ops monthly
- Prepare for SOC 2 Type II readiness (not certification at this stage, but the infrastructure to support it)

### Technical Hiring & Team Building
- Write the engineering hiring plan: who to hire next, in what order, at what level
- Conduct technical phone screens and take-home evaluations for all engineering candidates
- Define engineering quality standards: code style, review requirements, documentation standards, test coverage expectations

### Investor & Enterprise Support
- Prepare the Technical Due Diligence Package: architecture docs, scalability analysis, security posture, risk assessment
- Attend investor meetings when technical depth is needed
- Answer enterprise prospect security questionnaires (with Ops and Legal Advisor support)
- Build the enterprise dashboard prototype when Sales needs it for demos

### Research-to-Production Pipeline
- Translate the Behavioral Neuroscientist's research into technical specifications
- Translate the NRI™ framework's 9-circuit model into implementable engine specifications
- Ensure the voice pipeline implements the prosody-to-nervous-system requirements from Research
- Support patent filings by documenting technical innovations and providing technical declarations

## 4. Senior AI/ML Engineer

**The intelligence layer. Makes Siani actually smart.**

**Reports to:** CTO | **Direct reports:** None
**Core mandate:** Build the systems that make Siani intelligent: conversation engine, Signal Score computation, emotional/behavioral detection engines, and voice pipeline ML components. Ship models to production, not papers.

### Conversation Engine
- Build and maintain the conversation engine: LLM orchestration, context window management, multi-turn memory, mode switching logic
- Implement the 10 interaction modes as distinct behavioral configurations: Stabilize, Elevate, Challenge, Reflect, Hype, Decode, Clarify, Reframe, Ground, Move
- Design and implement the mode transition logic: what triggers a shift from Reflect to Move, how the engine detects when to switch
- Manage context windows: what to keep, what to summarize, what to forget
- Implement the prompt templates from the Product/Conv Designer as production-ready system prompts
- Optimize inference latency: Siani's text response must be generated within the allotted response generation budget (<200ms target)

### Signal Score & Behavioral Detection
- Implement Signal Score v1: compute Self-Trust, Agency, Regulation, Coherence, and Shame Load from conversational signals
- Work with Behavioral Neuroscientist to translate the psychometric model into computable features
- Build the first behavioral detection engines: emotional state classification, shame/avoidance signal extraction, cognitive load estimation, safety/crisis detection
- Implement the Anti-Dependency detection system: identify patterns that suggest unhealthy reliance vs. healthy engagement
- Validate all ML models against real user data

### Voice Pipeline ML
- Implement prosody feature extraction from audio: pitch, pace, energy, pause patterns, vocal tremor
- Implement emotion classification from audio: distress, calm, excitement, shutdown, anger, crying
- Optimize ML inference within the voice pipeline latency budget: prosody extraction <100ms, emotion classification <100ms
- Work with the Behavioral Neuroscientist on the prosody-to-nervous-system mapping
- Ensure the voice ML works across accents, dialects, code-switching, and non-standard speech patterns

### Data & Experimentation
- Build the data pipeline: conversation data → structured features → Signal Score computation → engine inputs
- Track all ML experiments in Weights & Biases: hypothesis, configuration, results, decision
- Create model cards for every deployed model: purpose, training data, performance metrics, known limitations, fairness evaluation
- Design and run A/B tests for engine improvements
- Build the model evaluation framework: accuracy, latency, false positive/negative rates, real-world outcome correlation

## 6. Product & Conversation Designer

**Two jobs in one. Decides what gets built AND how Siani speaks.**

**Reports to:** CEO | **Direct reports:** None (will split into 2 roles post-seed)
**Core mandate:** Own the end-to-end consumer product experience: what features exist, how they work, how Siani talks, how the Crew behaves, what the user feels. The most artifact-heavy role in the company.

### Product Strategy & Roadmap
- Write the 3-year product vision document: what the Siani + Crew experience looks like in Year 1, 2, 3
- Own the quarterly product roadmap: prioritized by what proves the thesis, what generates enterprise data, and what creates user love
- Write every PRD using the standard template. At this stage, there is no other PM.
- Define the beta program: who gets access, in what order, what data to collect, how to iterate
- Build and present the investor product demo alongside the CEO
- Maintain the competitive analysis: Replika, Character.ai, Pi, Hume, Woebot. Updated monthly.

### Conversation Design: Siani
- Write the Siani Conversation Design Bible v1.0 from scratch. This is the founding document that governs how Siani speaks for years.
  - Personality profile: who Siani IS. Values, boundaries, humor, warmth, directness.
  - 10 interaction modes: Stabilize, Elevate, Challenge, Reflect, Hype, Decode, Clarify, Reframe, Ground, Move. Each with trigger conditions, entry behavior, tone, examples, exit criteria, contraindications.
  - Mirror mechanics: how Siani reflects back what the user said in a way that feels seen, not parroted
  - Lifestyle Elevation Language Dictionary: clinical-to-SAINTE translation (anxiety → "head running hot," depression → "running on empty")
  - Silence Architecture: pause durations by context. Post-shame disclosure ≥2.8s. Post-celebration 1.2s.
  - Turn-taking rules: when Siani speaks, when she waits, when she interrupts (almost never)
  - Culture-coded calibration: pragmatic language calibration, NOT slang injection
  - Hard rules and exclusions: what Siani will NEVER say, topics she redirects, unsafe disclosures protocol
- Write the prompt engineering templates that implement the conversation design. At this stage, this person writes the actual LLM prompts.
- Design the onboarding conversation flow: first 30 seconds, first 5 minutes, first 3 sessions. The Slow Reveal.
- Maintain the Edge Case Decision Log: DV disclosure, suicidal ideation, child user, intoxicated user, legal trouble.

### Conversation Design: The Crew
- Write personality profiles for the first 3-5 Crew agents (Scout, Haven, Maven, Anchor): voice, tone, vocabulary, energy, humor, boundaries, domain expertise
- Design the handoff experience: when Siani says "I got someone who knows about this" and introduces a Crew member. Must feel like a friend introducing a friend, not a phone tree transfer. Write 5-10 variations per handoff.
- Design the canDoWithYou interaction model: how Crew agents walk alongside users while building their independence.

### User Research
- Conduct all user research personally at this stage: interviews, observation, session replay review
- Talk to 5+ users per week during beta
- Write user research synthesis after every study
- Listen to session recordings daily. Know what the product feels like for real users.

### Metrics & Quality
- Define and track product KPIs: DAU, session depth, Signal Score trajectory, loop closure rate, NPS
- Establish the conversation audit practice: daily review of 10-20 real sessions for quality and personality consistency
- Review every conversation Siani has during early beta. Every single one.
- Conduct ethical review of every feature with the AI Ethics Lead before engineering begins

  - Serotonergic Circuit: how predictability and consistency create stability
  - Default Mode Network: how reflection modes engage self-referential processing

## 12. AI Ethics & Human-Centered Design Lead

**The moral architecture. The only role besides CEO that can block work.**

**Reports to:** CEO (not buried under engineering or legal) | **Direct reports:** None
**Core mandate:** Ensure SAINTE does what it claims: builds autonomy not dependency, creates safety not surveillance, generates trust not extraction. This person sits in product reviews, designs consent flows, defines AI behavior boundaries, and has veto power.

### Ethical Framework & Principles
- Write the SAINTE Ethical AI Framework v1.0: operationalized principles, not abstract values
  - Autonomy over dependency: every interaction should build user capacity, never create reliance
  - Presence over surveillance: the Dual-Reality Architecture must feel like being known, not being watched
  - Trust over extraction: data should serve the user first, the enterprise second, and never be sold
  - Dignity over efficiency: if a faster approach compromises the user's dignity, we take the slower approach
- These principles are not aspirational. They are engineering requirements.

### Anti-Dependency Protocol
- Define engagement thresholds: how many sessions per day/week before the system flags potential dependency
- Define dependency warning signals: what conversational patterns indicate unhealthy attachment
- Define intervention triggers: when Siani should gently redirect the user toward real-world relationships
- Define the design constraints that prevent dependency by design: progressive disclosure, deliberate friction points, celebration of user independence

### Consent Architecture
- Design consent flows for every data type: voice recording, conversational content, behavioral signals, Signal Score, ambient channel data, enterprise data sharing
- Ensure all consent is: explicit, informed (plain language), granular, and withdrawable (user can revoke at any time with actual data deletion)
- Design child/teen consent (COPPA + beyond): parental notification, age-appropriate language, developmental safeguards
- Design consent for ambient channels: smart speaker in a shared home — who consents? SMS — different privacy model.

### Feature Review & Veto
- Review every PRD before engineering begins using the Ethical Review Checklist
- Assess: dependency risk, surveillance risk, consent adequacy, cultural harm potential, data minimization, vulnerable population impact, algorithmic fairness
- Approve, approve with required changes, or block. Block = feature cannot proceed.
- **This is the only role besides CEO that has veto power over product decisions.**

### Participatory Design
- Establish an ongoing co-design program with community members: actual Medicaid recipients, justice-involved individuals, people with lived experience
- This is not a focus group. It's an ongoing partnership with compensation.
- Facilitate participatory design sessions at key product milestones: onboarding, first Crew interaction, consent flows, ambient channels

### Algorithmic Fairness & Safety
- Assess Signal Score computation for fairness: does it systematically score certain populations differently? (racial, geographic, linguistic, disability)
- Assess engine routing for fairness: does Siani respond differently to different populations in ways that create inequity?
- Define what data should NEVER be used for, and document this as a hard constraint shared with engineering
- Write the Child Safety Ethics Protocol: developmental appropriateness, parental transparency, anti-manipulation safeguards

### External Credibility
- Write the investor and enterprise ethics narrative: specific, credible explanation for decks and sales. Not "we care about ethics" — "here is exactly how our anti-dependency protocol works."
- Produce quarterly Ethics Audit: review product behavior, data practices, consent flows, and dependency metrics against the framework.
