## The Frame

We are not selling a product. We are offering a pilot that pays for itself.

Every MCO and FQHC we approach has the same problem: high-cost, low-engagement Medicaid members who spiral into ER visits because there's no consistent human (or human-feeling) relationship between appointments. We have 14% ER reduction data from a small cohort. That's the wedge. Everything else is context.

The decision-maker is never the person who picks up the phone. The decision-maker is the Chief Medical Officer, the VP of Population Health, or the Director of Behavioral Health. That's who we get to. How we get there is different for each prospect below.

The ask is simple: a 6-month paid pilot at $25 PMPM for a defined member cohort (500-2,000 high-risk members). Pilot success is defined up front. We deliver outcomes data. They decide on full contract.

## Discovery Call Framework

When we get in the room, we follow this sequence:

**1. Open with their problem (5 min)**
"Tell me about your highest-cost behavioral health population. What's happening with them between appointments?"

**2. Show them the gap we fill (5 min)**
"We call it the void between appointments. 90 million Medicaid members have no consistent relationship with healthcare between crises. That void is where ER utilization happens."

**3. Show the proof (5 min)**
"Here's what we've seen in our beta cohort: 84% DAU. 32% referral completion lift. 14% ER visit reduction. This is not a content app. This is relational continuity that produces measurable outcomes."

**4. Describe the pilot (5 min)**
"A 6-month pilot. 500-2,000 of your highest-risk behavioral health members. $25 PMPM. We define success metrics together up front. At the end, you have the data to make a full contract decision."

**5. Understand their constraints (10 min)**
"What would need to be true for this to work inside your organization? Who else needs to be in this conversation? What's your procurement timeline?"

**6. Next step (5 min)**
Always leave with a specific next step: second meeting, internal sponsor identified, or timeline for feedback.
