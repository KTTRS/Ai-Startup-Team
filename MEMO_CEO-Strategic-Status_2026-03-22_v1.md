## What We Have Decided

### On product
**We are voice-first.** Not text with a voice option. Voice-first. This was a decision worth making because it changes everything about how Siani feels — and how Siani works with populations that have been failed by text-based mental health tools.

**We are not a clinical tool.** SAINTE does not diagnose. SAINTE does not treat. SAINTE maintains the relational continuity that the healthcare system has structurally failed to provide. This distinction is intentional, legally important, and the reason we scale where clinical tools cannot.

**We are shame-aware by architecture.** Not as a feature. As a foundational design constraint. Everything Siani says is filtered through: does this activate shame? This is not a UI choice — it is the reason our retention numbers are what they are.

**The Crew rolls out after Siani v1 is proven.** We do not introduce complexity before the core relationship is established. Siani first. Crew second.

### On the business model
**We are B2B2C, always free to end user.** The user never pays. MCOs, health systems, and employers pay $25-30 PMPM. Value-based care bonuses on top. This model is non-negotiable — charging the user would violate the foundational ethos of the company and the trust of the communities we serve.

**The wedge is Medicaid MCOs.** 90 million members. Budget. Regulatory pressure. No good options. This is where we prove the model. Commercial and employer expansion comes after Medicaid credentialing.

**Pilots are defined by outcomes up front.** We do not run pilots without pre-defined success metrics. The pilot terms include: member cohort size, engagement benchmarks, outcome measures, and decision timeline for full contract. We do not do open-ended pilots.

### On the raise
**We are raising $2.5M at a $10M post-money cap.** Not less. A $1M bridge extends the crisis without solving it. We need a team. A team needs real capital.

**Lead investor committed by May 15.** If no lead by May 1, we activate a parallel strategy: non-dilutive grants, SBIR applications, strategic bridge from angels only. We do not wait past May 15 to make this call.

**We will not take money that compromises the product.** We will not take money from investors who want us to medicalize, monetize the user, or dilute the cultural integrity. Every check we take carries the story of why we took it.

### On the team
**We operate on agent infrastructure until the seed closes.** This is not ideal. It is the reality. The agent team is not a substitute for humans — it is the scaffolding that keeps us organized and producing until we can hire.

**First 3 hires post-seed:** Lead ML Engineer, Full-Stack Engineer, Head of Sales. These roles must be identified before the round closes so capital lands on people, not positions.

**Advisors must be formalized.** All 6 advisors get signed agreements with equity and defined deliverables before the seed closes. No more informal advisory relationships.

### On culture
**Culture is not a marketing strategy. Culture IS the infrastructure.** The Founding Voices program, SAINTE Creative, the Chapters, the Drops — these are not afterthoughts. They are the moat. The community IS the distribution. We build the movement while we build the product.

**First Chapter event happens in Q2.** Detroit or Atlanta. 50+ people minimum. Real content. Real community. Not a launch party — a gathering.

## What We Need to Decide

### Decision 1: Pilot pricing — $25 PMPM or tiered?
**Context:** The $25-30 PMPM is our stated model. For early pilots with FQHCs (which have tighter budgets than MCOs), this may be high. The question is whether we pilot at a lower rate to get case study data, or hold the price.

**The tension:** Lower price gets us data faster but sets a floor that's hard to raise. Full price filters for serious enterprise buyers but may slow the first deal.

**Recommendation:** Hold $25 PMPM for MCOs. For FQHCs, consider a "founding partner" rate of $15 PMPM with explicit understanding that full-rate contract follows after pilot success. Do not discount more than this.

**Decision needed by:** April 15 (before first enterprise discovery calls complete)
**Who decides:** CEO + Head of Sales Agent input

### Decision 2: State Medicaid vs. MCO vs. FQHC — first enterprise target?
**Context:** We have three entry paths. MCOs (Molina, Centene, Meridian) have the most money and the most complex procurement. FQHCs move faster but have less budget. State Medicaid is the longest procurement cycle but the biggest upside.

**The tension:** Speed vs. size. FQHC gets us a case study in 90 days. MCO gets us a $1M+ annual contract eventually. State gets us a mandate.

**Recommendation:** Pursue all three tracks simultaneously but allocate energy as: 50% MCO (Medicaid plans), 35% FQHC (Detroit-based first), 15% state (longer game, relationship-building now).

**Decision needed by:** April 1 (before we deploy enterprise sales energy)
**Who decides:** CEO + Head of Sales Agent

### Decision 3: Who leads the round — healthcare-focused fund or founder-conviction fund?
**Context:** We have two investor archetypes as lead candidates. Healthcare-focused funds (Flare Capital, 7wire, Rock Health) will do deep clinical diligence and open MCO doors. Founder-conviction funds (Kapor Capital, Collab Capital, Harlem Capital) will move faster and require less clinical proof.

**The tension:** Healthcare investors validate the clinical story but may slow the process or want clinical evidence we don't yet have at scale. Founder-conviction investors trust our judgment but may not open the MCO doors we need.

**Recommendation:** Lead with Kapor Capital and Harlem Capital outreach first (founder-conviction, faster decision). Simultaneously build relationships with 7wire and Flare Capital (healthcare, slower but strategic). If Kapor or Harlem leads, bring in a healthcare strategic investor for the round.

**Decision needed by:** May 1 (when lead investor must be in term discussion)
**Who decides:** CEO

### Decision 4: Public narrative timing — when do we start telling the SAINTE story publicly?
**Context:** We have Techstars Detroit, MIT Solve, Apple Developer Academy credibility. We have 84% DAU data. We have a cultural movement that exists but isn't visible yet. The question: do we go loud now (pre-seed, builds awareness) or stay quiet until the seed is closed (reduces noise, protects narrative for the right moment)?

**The tension:** Going public now builds the community and cultural gravity before the raise, which makes the raise easier. Going public too early with incomplete product could undermine credibility with enterprise buyers.

**Recommendation:** Controlled public presence starts now. Social content at 3x per week — culture, mission, team. No product claims we can't back up. No enterprise discussions referenced publicly. Founding Voices program activated. Big public announcement (with data) waits until first enterprise LOI is signed.

**Decision needed by:** March 28
**Who decides:** CEO + Brand Agent

### Decision 5: Legal structure — are we set up correctly for a SAFE round?
**Context:** We need to confirm: Delaware C-Corp status, clean cap table, IP assignments for all founders and contributors, no conflicts or outstanding legal liabilities, BAA template ready for enterprise.

**What we know:** $300K+ raised to date — we have a cap table. Techstars means we've been through basic legal setup. But we need to confirm everything is clean before we open a data room.

**Decision needed by:** April 7 (data room complete deadline)
**Who decides:** CEO + Legal Advisor + Ops Agent
**Immediate action:** Legal Advisor reviews cap table and incorporation docs; Ops Agent builds data room structure; CEO reviews and signs off.

## What I'm Most Worried About

**Not the product.** The product works. The traction proves it.

**Not the market.** 90 million Medicaid members with no relational continuity is not a niche. The market is real and the problem is structural.

**What I'm worried about:**

1. **Speed of the enterprise cycle.** MCO procurement takes 6-18 months. We may not be able to close a LOI before the seed round closes — which means we're raising on traction and thesis, not signed contracts. This is manageable but requires the right investor.

2. **Clinical credibility at scale.** Our 14% ER reduction data is real but comes from a small cohort. Enterprise medical directors will ask: "How many patients? What was the control?" We need to be completely honest about the size and methodology while being completely confident in the direction of the signal.

3. **Runway timing vs. raise timing.** $0 runway means we cannot negotiate from strength. Every investor knows this. The traction is the counter — 84% DAU says "this team can execute." But we need to close fast, which limits our ability to wait for the perfect terms.

4. **Culture and product coherence at speed.** We are building a cultural movement and a clinical-grade enterprise product simultaneously. The tension is real. The brand voice that resonates in community has to translate to the language a CMO trusts. We manage this by being clear: Siani speaks human. SAINTE speaks enterprise. Both are true.
